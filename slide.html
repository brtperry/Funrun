  • The Course

    The course is a distance of 10 kilometres. Starting at the Hilton Hotel {A}, heading south along the promenade past Blackpool Tower, The Golden Mile, and Blackpool Pleasure Beach to the world's largest Mirror Ball {B}. It then turns to return along the promenade and finishes outside the Hilton Hotel on the Middle Walk. Water stations will be situated at approximately quarter, half and three quarter distances, as well as at the finish.

    The area around the Hilton Hotel and the race start can get very busy on the day, please therefore ensure you allow time to find suitable parking. 3 chargeable car parks have been identified on the map.

    Best place to park is Central Parking. From the promenade catch a tram to the Hilton Hotel.

  • Registration

    To secure your place in the run, we advise entering online. You can register online now by completing the online entry form. The entry fee is £11.00 for adults aged 16 and over and £8.50 for children aged 13—15. Running packs including your race number and all relevant information will be sent out from the beginning of March.

    If you'd prefer to register on the day, this can be done via the Late Check—In desk at the Hilton Hotel from 9.30am onwards. Please ensure you leave enough time before the race starts.

    Facilities

    The HQ for the event is the Hilton Hotel, North Promenade Blackpool.

    The hotel kindly provides changing facilities, toilets and a place to leave belongings. Whilst every care is taken to ensure belongings are kept safe, the Hilton Hotel and the organisers of the event cannot be held responsible for any loss or damage sustained.

    Refreshments will be on sale at the hotel throughout the day.

    Serious Runners

    For safety reasons, there will be a section at the front of the Start line for serious runners expecting to complete the course in less than 40 minutes. Please do not line up in this area unless you expect to finish in under 40 minutes.

    Racing Wheelchairs and Rollerskates

    There is a special start for racing wheelchair entrants at 10.50 am (10 minutes prior to the main Start time).

    In the interests of safety we regret that roller skaters will not be allowed to participate.

    Transport

    Blackpool is accessible by all main transport methods. Train stations are located at Blackpool North and Blackpool South with regular services operating between each of the two (please use Blackpool North if travelling by train to the Fun Run).

    A frequent and reliable bus and tram service are also operated in Blackpool town centre, for full details visit Blackpool Gov

    Medical Facilities

    St John's Ambulance personnel will be in attendance along the course and at the finish.

    There will also be a rescue bus following runners. If you find yourself in any difficulty, please wait for the rescue bus and you will be taken back to the start area.

    The Finish

    Marshals will log your race number as you pass through the finish line. To ensure your correct position and time are printed on the results, you must ensure you retain your position when entering the lanes after the finish, displaying your race number clearly.

    Upon completion of the course, every runner will receive a Limited Edition Medal. A full results list will be published in the Gazette on Monday 14th May, as well as on this website.

Beaverbrooks Blackpool 10K, Celebrating 30 Years

The Beaverbrooks Blackpool 10K Fun Run first started in 1983 and has grown in popularity ever since. Attracting thousands of runners each year, the event is organised by the St Annes Coordination of Charities along with Beaverbrooks the Jewellers, The Blackpool Gazette, Radio Wave and the Hilton Hotel, all of whom have provided overwhelming and fundamental support over the last 30 years. The event also wouldn't be possible without the help of various other individuals and organisations including Blackpool Tourism Department, the local Police force, and the 200 marshalls that give up their time on a voluntary basis.

Each year the chosen charity changes and we're extremely proud to have raised over £1.5 million for charities including Cancer Research UK, Manchester Royal Children's Hospital, Trinity Hospice, Brian House Children's Hospice and Blackpool Victoria Hospital for the Blue Skies & Prostate Cancer Appeals. This money has helped to save lives both in the local area and beyond. In addition to these chosen charities, the Fun Run is also a great way for local charities to raise money; organisers estimate that this additional fundraising takes the total figure to over £3 million. We couldn't have done this without your support and so would like to say a massive THANK YOU. Please help us to continue raising money and saving lives by taking part, donating or fundraising. Together let's make 2012 the biggest year yet and celebrate 30 years in style.

From all of us here at the charity, thanks in advance for your support.
